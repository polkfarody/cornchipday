# Cornchip Day — Character Bible

## Cornchip (Protagonist / Playable Character)
- **Silhouette:** An upside-down triangle, corn-chip textured, with comedic articulated legs, expressive eyes, and a mouth.
- **Role:** Player character. Runs, jumps, and collects ingredients across 7 levels to win back his brother.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Plucky, determined, food-pun-driven optimist; treats every obstacle as a minor, laughable setback rather than a threat, consistent with the game's no-fail-state design philosophy.

## Wrap (Cornchip's Brother)
- **Silhouette:** A rounded, wrap/burrito-shaped body with comedic articulated legs, expressive eyes, and a mouth.
- **Role:** Cornchip's brother and the source of each level's obstacles — thrown pun ingredients ("mild" salsa, vegetarian cheese, etc.). Confirmed as the game's final boss, fought after all other levels' ingredients are collected (each level has its own boss guarding that level's ingredient; see "Level Bosses" below).
- **Narrative Function:** Estranged from Cornchip after an argument. Reconciliation occurs once Cornchip has collected all 7 ingredients and presents them to make "the ultimate crunch wrap."
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Proud and stubborn on the surface; the obstacle-throwing reads as wounded-sibling antics rather than genuine villainy, and he wants to reconcile as much as Cornchip does.

## Cheeto (Post-MVP)
- **Role:** Cornchip's best friend; planned second playable character for a future local co-op mode.
- **Status:** Concept only — no visual design or mechanics required for MVP.

## Level Bosses
Each level culminates in a boss battle guarding that level's ingredient; Wrap is the final boss, fought after all other levels' ingredients are collected (see `game-brief.txt` Level & Narrative Structure).

### Hot Sauce
- **Silhouette:** A happy squeeze bottle whose cap doubles as a sombrero; has cartoon legs and a face.
- **Role:** Level boss. Fires sauce projectiles from the opening in his hat, styled visually like lava — escalating the "mild salsa" joke from the base game brief.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Fiery, enthusiastic showoff.

### Avocado
- **Silhouette:** A dancing avocado.
- **Role:** Level boss. Dances and jumps rhythmically; each landing drops a puddle of guacamole that Cornchip cannot touch — a hazard-terrain attack rather than a direct projectile.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Groovy, carefree, always mid-dance.

### Salsa Bowl
- **Silhouette:** A rolling bowl of salsa, mounted on small corn-shaped wheels.
- **Role:** Fires tomato chunks at Cornchip while rolling around.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Not yet specified.
- **Open question:** Is Salsa Bowl a full level boss (like Hot Sauce/Avocado) or a regular enemy encountered within a level?

## Regular Enemies
Smaller, one-hit threats encountered within a level rather than guarding its ingredient. Unlike bosses, they don't patrol as far, don't attack at range, and go down in a single stomp.

### Cheese
- **Silhouette:** A friendly wedge of yellow cheese with cartoon legs, a sleepy/mischievous expression, and visible cheese holes -- real art generated (`cheese_enemy_frame1-8.png`), no longer a placeholder.
- **Role:** Regular enemy (resolved from the earlier boss-vs-enemy open question). Now built with its own signature effect: touch puts Cornchip to sleep (input disabled, no life lost) rather than the generic hit stun -- see `feature.md` F6. Originally spec'd at 10 seconds; tuned down to 3 seconds after testing felt too punishing.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Not yet specified.

## Bestiary by Level
**Confirmed by the user** as the roster/order for Levels 2-7, including keeping Queso Grande and Jalapeño as-is. Originally written up unilaterally without checking each character first (see instructions-ai.txt Operating Principle 4) -- flagged, reviewed, and approved as a whole rather than being silently treated as decided.

Resolves the long-standing open question of what each of the 7 levels is themed around, and answers the "the level feels repetitive" note by having each level introduce at least one brand-new enemy, with any returning enemy coming back tougher or with a new trick rather than just reappearing unchanged.

### Level 1 — Salsa Cantina (built)
A fun Mexican-restaurant vibe; the onboarding level, kept simple so the theme and core controls land before anything harder shows up.
- **Boss:** Hot Sauce (3-hit, fires a projectile)
- **Enemies:** Cheese (1-hit, melee only), Salsa Bowl (2-hit, fires a projectile)

### Level 2 — Nacho Kitchen (built)
- **Boss:** Queso Grande — a bubbling cheese-fondue pot. Attacks by dripping a cheese glob that sticks and briefly slows Cornchip rather than an instant hit, a new hazard *type* rather than another projectile.
- **Enemies:** Cheese returns, with its sleep effect (see Regular Enemies above). Jalapeño, a hopping enemy that's otherwise a standard side-hit stun.
- **Palette/setting:** tiled nacho bar, cheese-yellow.

### Level 3 — Guac Stand (built)
- **Boss:** Avocado. Defeat condition confirmed by the user: jump-stomp, same as every other boss -- the guac-puddle drops are terrain to dodge between hits, not part of how he's defeated.
- **Enemies:** Lime (user-specified, additions.txt) — a wedge of lime that squirts toxic lime juice at Cornchip, a ranged attack (standard hit/stun like other projectiles, reuses `BossProjectile.tscn`). Onion — a stationary enemy (`boss.gd`'s `move_speed = 0`) whose nearby presence briefly wobbles the screen (new `wobble_radius` export + `player.gd`'s `apply_screen_wobble()`, a camera-offset jitter reusing the run-bob's sine-wave technique) rather than costing a life; still stompable like any regular enemy on direct contact.
- **Palette/setting:** produce stand, greens.
- **Onion's missing fume-line decoration** (2 of 8 generated frames, see `plan.md` Phase 1): user confirmed ship as-is rather than attempt a crop-script recovery or pay to regenerate.

### Level 4 — Market Tomatoes (built)
- **Boss:** Big Red — 3 stomps crack him apart into two Cherry Tomato enemies (`boss.gd`'s new `split_into_scene`/`split_count`/`split_offset_x` exports) rather than dying normally; the ingredient only drops once both pieces are defeated (`shared_defeat_group` export -- a piece leaves the group immediately on defeat and drops the ingredient only if no group-mate remains). Confirmed by the user before building.
- **Enemies:** Cherry Tomato (small, fast, 1-hit, melee-only -- appears both as a standalone enemy and as what Big Red spawns), Salsa Bowl returns tougher (`fire_interval` lowered from 4.0 to 2.5 on the existing scene, no new code).
- **Palette/setting:** outdoor market stalls, reds.
- **Art note:** Cherry Tomato's first generation batch came back with readable text labels baked into every pose (a real defect, not shipped) -- regenerated clean with `gemini-3-pro-image`, same fix as the earlier F11 text-defect batch. Big Red's grid had the same "extra pose" anomaly as Lime's (10 frames instead of 8, from the crack-apart sequence rendering as disconnected blobs) -- used frames 1,2,3,4 (idle/move) plus 7 (dizzy, as the normal hit reaction) and 8 (fully-cracked, used as the split moment), skipped the redundant 5/6/9/10.

### Level 5 — Frosty Fridge (Sour Cream) (built)
- **Boss:** Sour Cream Sam — has no projectile/hazard attack at all; per spec, the icy floor *is* the challenge. New `IceZone.tscn` (an `Area2D` covering the arena) toggles a new `player.gd` state (`is_on_ice`) on entry/exit; while true, horizontal movement uses `move_toward` (a gradual ramp) instead of the normal instant velocity snap, so Cornchip drifts/slides rather than stopping and turning on a dime. Reusable for any future arena-changes-the-fight boss (e.g. Level 6's heating floor).
- **Enemies:** Ice Cube (new, 1-hit, fast -- highest `move_speed` of any enemy so far). Chive Bit (new, 1-hit, tiny -- smallest scale of any character so far, placed in a cluster of 3 for the "small swarms" description rather than any new spawner code).
- **Palette/setting:** walk-in fridge, cool blues.

### Level 6 — Sizzling Griddle (Crunchy Shell) (built)
- **Boss:** Iron Skillet — no attack of any kind, same as Sour Cream Sam; the arena floor itself is the challenge. New `HeatZone.tscn`/`heat_zone.gd` (same family as `IceZone.tscn`): cycles safe (4s) -> warning flash (0.8s) -> hot (2s) -> repeat, hurting any grounded player inside it during the hot phase ("can't stand still"). Design confirmed with the user before building, including the warning-flash fairness detail for the target 5-8 year old audience. Verified via headless script: exactly one hit per hot phase (a latch resets each cycle), correct phase timing.
- **Enemies:** Jalapeño returns tougher (2-hit instead of 1, faster). Onion returns tougher (`wobble_radius` 100 -> 140). Grease Splatter (new) is the first enemy that genuinely cannot be stomped -- `boss.gd`'s new `cannot_be_stomped` export skips the stomp-from-above check entirely, so any contact (including from above) hurts the player instead of defeating it; the Air Fryer spin-dash is the one thing that still defeats it, matching its established "defeats anything you touch" rule with no stated exception. Verified via headless script: a stomp-shaped contact leaves it undamaged and stuns the player, spin-dash still defeats it.
- **Double jump unlocked starting this level** (see `feature.md` F16) -- added after direct playtest feedback that Grease Splatter, even stationary, was genuinely too tall for a single jump to clear (confirmed with the actual jump-arc math, not just a guess). A permanent new ability from here on, not a pickup.
- **Palette/setting:** kitchen grill station, reds/oranges.

### Level 7 — The Wrap (Finale) (built)
No separate ingredient-boss precedes this one. Tone, resolved by direct user design: not combat at all. A remix gauntlet (one returning enemy per Level 1-6: Cheese, Jalapeño, Lime, Cherry Tomato, Ice Cube, Grease Splatter -- no new assets) leads to an arena where Wrap stands, no health, no attack, not fought. Six delivery pickups (the same `Ingredient`/`CheeseIngredient`/`GuacIngredient`/`TomatoIngredient`/`SourCreamIngredient`/`CrunchyShellIngredient` scenes already built for Levels 1-6, just placed as tokens in this arena) are collected by walking into them, same interaction as any pickup. Reaching Level 7 at all already proves all 6 were earned, since the level chain only advances on ingredient collection -- no new cross-level save state needed. Once all 6 are delivered, Wrap lights up (his existing "warmer, softer smile" pose); jumping onto him (a stomp-shaped landing, matching every other boss's detection) completes the ultimate crunch wrap and ends the game in a simple in-place celebration -- see `feature.md` F17.
Double jump (F16) is carried forward into this level (`grants_double_jump = true`) since it's meant to be permanent from Level 6 onward.
