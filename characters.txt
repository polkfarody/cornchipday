# Cornchip Day — Character Bible

## Cornchip (Protagonist / Playable Character)
- **Silhouette:** An upside-down triangle, corn-chip textured, with comedic articulated legs, expressive eyes, and a mouth.
- **Role:** Player character. Runs, jumps, and collects ingredients across 7 levels to win back his brother.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Plucky, determined, food-pun-driven optimist; treats every obstacle as a minor, laughable setback rather than a threat, consistent with the game's no-fail-state design philosophy.

## Wrap (Cornchip's Brother)
- **Silhouette:** A rounded, wrap/burrito-shaped body with comedic articulated legs, expressive eyes, and a mouth.
- **Role:** Cornchip's brother and the source of each level's obstacles — thrown pun ingredients ("mild" salsa, vegetarian cheese, etc.). Confirmed as the game's final boss, fought after all other levels' ingredients are collected (each level has its own boss guarding that level's ingredient; see "Level Bosses" below).
- **Narrative Function:** Estranged from Cornchip after an argument. Reconciliation occurs once Cornchip has collected all 7 ingredients and presents them to make "the ultimate crunch wrap."
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Proud and stubborn on the surface; the obstacle-throwing reads as wounded-sibling antics rather than genuine villainy, and he wants to reconcile as much as Cornchip does.

## Cheeto (Post-MVP)
- **Role:** Cornchip's best friend; planned second playable character for a future local co-op mode.
- **Status:** Concept only — no visual design or mechanics required for MVP.

## Level Bosses
Each level culminates in a boss battle guarding that level's ingredient; Wrap is the final boss, fought after all other levels' ingredients are collected (see `game-brief.txt` Level & Narrative Structure).

### Hot Sauce
- **Silhouette:** A happy squeeze bottle whose cap doubles as a sombrero; has cartoon legs and a face.
- **Role:** Level boss. Fires sauce projectiles from the opening in his hat, styled visually like lava — escalating the "mild salsa" joke from the base game brief.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Fiery, enthusiastic showoff.

### Avocado
- **Silhouette:** A dancing avocado.
- **Role:** Level boss. Dances and jumps rhythmically; each landing drops a puddle of guacamole that Cornchip cannot touch — a hazard-terrain attack rather than a direct projectile.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Groovy, carefree, always mid-dance.

### Salsa Bowl
- **Silhouette:** A rolling bowl of salsa, mounted on small corn-shaped wheels.
- **Role:** Fires tomato chunks at Cornchip while rolling around.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Not yet specified.
- **Open question:** Is Salsa Bowl a full level boss (like Hot Sauce/Avocado) or a regular enemy encountered within a level?

## Regular Enemies
Smaller, one-hit threats encountered within a level rather than guarding its ingredient. Unlike bosses, they don't patrol as far, don't attack at range, and go down in a single stomp.

### Cheese
- **Silhouette:** Not yet specified — currently uses placeholder-generation art matching the general "food character with cartoon legs and a face" template.
- **Role:** Regular enemy (resolved from the earlier boss-vs-enemy open question). Now built with its own signature effect: touch puts Cornchip to sleep for 10 seconds (input disabled, no life lost) rather than the generic hit stun -- see `feature.md` F6.
- **Personality (draft — pending confirmation):** Not yet specified.

## Bestiary by Level
**This entire section, beyond Level 1 which is already built, is an AI-invented proposal — not a confirmed design.** Every named character, boss mechanic, and level theme from Level 2 onward was written up unilaterally in response to a request to plan out enemy variety, without checking each one with the user first. That was a mistake (see instructions-ai.txt Operating Principle 4) — nothing here should be treated as decided, and nothing further should be built from it without going through the concepts together first.

Original intent, kept for context: resolve the long-standing open question of what each of the 7 levels is themed around, and answer the "the level feels repetitive" note by having each level introduce at least one brand-new enemy, with any returning enemy coming back tougher or with a new trick rather than just reappearing unchanged.

### Level 1 — Salsa Cantina (built)
A fun Mexican-restaurant vibe; the onboarding level, kept simple so the theme and core controls land before anything harder shows up.
- **Boss:** Hot Sauce (3-hit, fires a projectile)
- **Enemies:** Cheese (1-hit, melee only), Salsa Bowl (2-hit, fires a projectile)

### Level 2 — Nacho Kitchen
- **Boss (new, unconfirmed):** Queso Grande — a bubbling cheese-fondue pot. Attacks by dripping a cheese glob that sticks and briefly slows Cornchip rather than an instant hit, a new hazard *type* rather than another projectile. **Art has already been generated for this character (a real cost was incurred) before checking whether the user wants it at all.**
- **Enemies:** Cheese returns, now with its "asleep for 10 seconds" effect (already built, see Regular Enemies above). New, unconfirmed: Jalapeño, a hopping enemy that's otherwise a standard side-hit stun — **art already generated for this one too, same caveat as Queso Grande.**
- **Palette/setting:** tiled nacho bar, cheese-yellow.

### Level 3 — Guac Stand
- **Boss:** Avocado (already designed/generated)
- **Enemies:** Lime (user-specified, additions.txt) — a wedge of lime that squirts toxic lime juice at Cornchip, a ranged attack (presumably the standard hit/stun like other projectiles unless told otherwise). Replaces an earlier AI-invented version of this character that had it bouncing around and reversing Cornchip's controls -- that idea is dropped now that a real spec exists. Onion (still AI-invented/unconfirmed) — a stationary enemy whose nearby presence blurs/wobbles the screen briefly (a nuisance, not a hit).
- **Palette/setting:** produce stand, greens.

### Level 4 — Market Tomatoes
- **Boss (new):** Big Red — a giant tomato that splits into two Cherry Tomato enemies partway through the fight, an evolving-fight-pattern boss rather than Hot Sauce's single-phase loop.
- **Enemies:** Cherry Tomato (new — small, fast, 1-hit; also the thing Big Red spawns), Salsa Bowl returns tougher (faster fire rate).
- **Palette/setting:** outdoor market stalls, reds.

### Level 5 — Frosty Fridge (Sour Cream)
- **Boss (new):** Sour Cream Sam — the arena floor turns icy/slippery for this fight, changing how Cornchip's movement feels rather than adding another attack type.
- **Enemies (new):** Ice Cube — fast, low-health, slides back and forth. Chive Bit — tiny, appears in small swarms.
- **Palette/setting:** walk-in fridge, cool blues.

### Level 6 — Sizzling Griddle (Crunchy Shell)
- **Boss (new):** Iron Skillet — the whole arena floor periodically heats up (can't stand still), an escalation of Sour Cream Sam's floor-changes-the-fight idea.
- **Enemies:** Jalapeño and Onion return tougher (explicit callback, not brand new) alongside one new one: Grease Splatter — hazard-only, cannot be stomped, must be avoided entirely (a deliberately different interaction type from every stompable enemy so far).
- **Palette/setting:** kitchen grill station, reds/oranges.

### Level 7 — The Wrap (Finale)
No separate ingredient-boss precedes this one — the level is built entirely around the Wrap confrontation. A short remix gauntlet of one enemy from each earlier level (no new assets needed) leads into Wrap himself as the true final boss. Tone (straight combat vs. a gentler reconciliation moment) is still an open question — see `game-brief.txt`.
