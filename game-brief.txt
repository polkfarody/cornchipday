# Cornchip Day — Game Design Brief

## Overview
- **Working Title:** Cornchip Day
- **Genre:** 2D single-player platformer
- **Engine:** Godot 4
- **Platform (MVP):** Desktop, keyboard input
- **Target Audience:** Children ages 5–8, playing solo or with light parental supervision
- **Player Count (MVP):** 1 (local co-op is a post-MVP stretch goal — see Future Scope)

## Elevator Pitch
Cornchip, a walking corn chip, sets out across seven pun-filled levels to track down his estranged brother Wrap and win him back — collecting the ingredients for the ultimate crunch wrap along the way.

## Premise
Cornchip and Wrap had an argument and split up. To reconcile, Cornchip must cross seven levels, each themed around an ingredient needed to build "the ultimate crunch wrap." Wrap, hurt by the falling-out, pelts Cornchip with obstacles throughout the journey — played for laughs rather than menace (e.g. "mild" salsa, vegetarian cheese). At the end of each level, Cornchip collects that level's ingredient; collecting all seven and presenting them to Wrap is what wins his brother back.

## Design Philosophy
The primary players are 5 and 8 years old. Every design decision should be filtered through:
- **No fail state.** Obstacles interrupt progress (a stumble, a gentle respawn) but never end the game or punish the player. Confidence and forward momentum matter more than challenge.
- **No reading required.** All instructions, feedback, and story beats are communicated through animation, icons, and visual staging — not text.
- **Humor is physical, not verbal.** The pun-based obstacles (mild salsa, vegetarian cheese, etc.) land through exaggerated visual comedy — a wilting reaction, a comedic slip — so the joke works even for a player who can't read the label.

## Core Gameplay Loop
1. Run and jump through a level, reacting to thrown obstacles and enemies — jumping on an enemy defeats it, Cornchip's baseline combat ability.
2. Reach the end of the level and defeat that level's boss (e.g. Hot Sauce, Avocado) to earn its ingredient.
3. Periodically unlock a new movement ability, required to access later levels.
4. Repeat across all 7 levels; the final level's boss is Wrap himself, and defeating him with all 7 ingredients in hand triggers the reconciliation ending.

## Player Abilities & Progression
- **Starting abilities:** run, jump.
- **Unlockable upgrades:** earned progressively across levels; each new ability is required to progress (i.e., gates level access rather than being purely optional), keeping a metroidvania-lite sense of growth without adding failure risk.

## Level & Narrative Structure
- **7 levels total**, each tied to one ingredient for the ultimate crunch wrap.
- Each level introduces or riffs on a food-based obstacle pun consistent with a "world of a walking corn chip," and ends with a boss battle against that ingredient's themed guardian (see `characters.txt` "Level Bosses" — Hot Sauce, Avocado, Cheese, and others still to be designed).
- Wrap is present narratively throughout and is the final boss, fought after all other levels' ingredients are collected.

## Art & Audio Direction
- **Art pipeline:** AI-generated sprites, iterated toward a single consistent visual style across characters and levels.
- **Character silhouettes:** simple geometric comedy — Cornchip as an upside-down triangle, Wrap as a rounded cylinder — both with cartoon limbs, eyes, and mouths (full detail in `characters.txt`).

## MVP Scope
A single, fully polished vertical-slice level demonstrating: core run/jump movement, one obstacle type, and the end-of-level ingredient-collection loop. Full 7-level progression and ability-gating follow post-MVP.

## Future Scope (Post-MVP)
- **2-player local co-op:** Cornchip's best friend Cheeto joins the quest as a second playable character. Explicitly out of scope for MVP; the codebase should avoid decisions that would make adding a second player unusually difficult later (see `instructions-ai.txt` extensibility principle).

## Open Questions
- Full boss roster and per-level order across all 7 levels — Hot Sauce and Avocado are confirmed candidates; Cheese's status as boss vs. regular enemy is still undecided.
- How do boss battles and the jump-to-defeat mechanic square with the no-fail-state pillar? Working assumption: losing to a boss just means an immediate retry with no penalty, same as any other obstacle — worth confirming explicitly.
- Is the Wrap confrontation framed the same as other boss battles (combat-first), or does it shift tone toward a gentler final reconciliation moment, given the sibling-reunion story?
- Should each level map to a specific real-world crunch wrap ingredient (lettuce, cheese, salsa, etc.), and if so, is there a preferred list/order?
- Any preferred reference art/style touchstones for the sprite-generation pipeline?
